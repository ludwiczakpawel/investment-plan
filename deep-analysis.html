üîÑ Rebalancing Rules

What is rebalancing? Selling overperformers and buying underperformers to maintain your target allocation. Sounds counterintuitive, but it's "sell high, buy low" discipline.

When to Rebalance

TriggerActionFrequency
Calendar-based Review allocation every 6-12 months Twice a year (June, December)
Threshold-based If any asset drifts >5% from target, rebalance As needed
Example: Target 40% World, now 50% ‚Üí Sell 10% World, buy underweight assets ‚Äî

How to Rebalance (Tax-Smart)

  1. Prioritize new money: If you're adding 1,000 PLN/month, direct it toward underweight assets first
  2. Rebalance in IKE first: No tax on selling winners there
  3. Avoid selling in regular account: Triggers 19% Belka tax. Only rebalance there if drift is extreme (>10%)
  4. Use dividends/cash: If any ETFs pay dividends, reinvest into underweight positions
Example Scenario: XAIX (AI) rallies +50%, now 30% of portfolio instead of 20%. You could sell some in IKE (tax-free), or just stop buying it and let other assets catch up over time.
